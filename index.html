How to be professional on social media

How to build a professional presence on social media

Time: 1 hour - indefinitely

Are you looking to build a presence on social media? Are you wondering how you can make your accounts look more clean and professional to employers and potential followers? Here are some simple steps to get you started!

  • Things You'll Need

  • Photo editing software
  • Phone
  • Professional photos (Headshots)

  1. Post photos of yourself that you consider to be professional

    These photos do not need to be professionally taken, and they don't even need to be professional headshots - though they should be for your profile photo if it is a LinkedIn or business account - but they should be appropriate and generally professional. Use editing software like Adobe Photoshop to clean up the background or enhance the image. And make sure that all images are appropriate. Would you want your mom or boss to see this image? If not, then you probably shouldn't post it.

  2. Post statements you'd feel comfortable sharing with your boss or clients in person

    It's easy to get lost in the toxic twittersphere and lose yourself to your intrusive thoughts. However, it's also extremely common nowadays for employers to look at a potential hire's social media accounts to make sure that they are appropriate and are who they say they are. Therefore, if you wouldn't say something at work to your boss or coworkers, then you probably shouldn't put it on social media, either. Make sure that your online presence is an accurate reflection of who you are in real life, too.

    Always consider what you're going to post before you post it. Create a filter for social media in your head.

  3. Hide friend's comments or information that is deemed to be inappropriate

    Although it's great to connect with your friends on social media, it's important to remember that not everybody is focussed on keeping their accounts professional, including our close friends. Look at your social media account from the viewpoint of an employer, and if a friend's inappropriate reposts or comments appear on your feed, you may want to delete them.

  4. Never comment about work online

    It's a good rule of thumb to not talk about work on any of your social media accounts - whether the statement you're making is good or bad. Future employers won't want to hire you if they think that there is a possibility you will trash talk their company, and current employers don't want promotions or demotions to come from their employees, but rather from their customers. Keep your work life private.

  5. Only follow, friend or repost people you know and trust

    I know, I know, it's been ingrained into our heads since the dawn of the iPhone. But it really is important for much more than safety. Employers or bosses can see who you follow and who you are mutuals with. Although it is important to make numerous connections, it is also important to ensure that those connections are credible and professional. Although following celebrities and other companies is OK, it's important not to make the wrong connections.

  6. Try to be as grammatically correct as possible

    Although it does not seem like the most important step, it is something that not a lot of people think about. Especially on professional or business accounts, it never hurts to flex your knowledge and show everyone that you can be professional and clear in every day life, not just at work.
